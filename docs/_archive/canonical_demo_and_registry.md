## A. Canonical 3-Minute Demo Flow

**Replaces:** `demo_strategy.md` §3 ("The 3-Minute Narrative Flow") and §4 ("The Ruthless Cut Hierarchy"), and the demo-sequence portions of `user_journey.md` Stage 2 and `narration_script.md`.

The demo backbone is three beats, fixed in this order, never reduced:

| Time | Beat | What Happens |
|:---|:---|:---|
| 0:00–0:15 | Hook | Land on Home. State the core line: *"Football's biggest controversies aren't about what happened. They're about what a word means."* |
| 0:15–1:10 | Beat 1 — Perišić | Decision 1 (fast, confident). Perspectives (Fan/Referee/VAR/Rulebook). Reveal: "deliberately" / "unnaturally bigger" are undefined. Decision 2. |
| 1:10–1:40 | Beat 2 — VAR Nested | Same incident, second pass — no need to re-explain setup. Reframe: "the correction mechanism has the same problem." Reveal: "clear and obvious" is undefined. Runs faster than Beat 1 since context is already established. |
| 1:40–2:50 | Beat 3 — Suárez | Decision 1. Perspectives. Reveal, named explicitly as a *different kind* of gap: "the law doesn't just leave words undefined — here it leaves the whole question of an adequate remedy unaddressed." Decision 2 / closing reflection — this is the line the judge should leave the room with. |
| 2:50–3:00 | Close | One sentence button-up. Mention (do not demo) that De Jong and Mbappé exist in the product as two further categories of the same phenomenon, explorable after the formal demo or during Q&A. |

**Cut hierarchy (single tier):** If time is short, cut Layer 2 (Live Generation / `/live`) entirely — do not open that page. The 3-beat backbone is never cut. If genuinely desperate, compress Beat 2 (VAR Nested) to a single spoken sentence instead of a full perspectives/reveal cycle — do not skip it outright, since it is the project's most distinctive insight, not a disposable extra.

## B. Canonical Incident Sequencing

**Replaces:** the "De Jong is 2nd in sequence, Mbappé is 3rd, Suárez is 4th" framing in `incident_registry.md`, which conflated product order with demo order.

There are two separate sequences. Both are correct, for different purposes:

**Product taxonomy order** (all 5, for in-app free exploration and the written submission's taxonomy claim):
1. Perišić — Intent/Threshold blend
2. De Jong — Threshold
3. Mbappé — Scope
4. VAR Nested — Judgment
5. Suárez — Context (climax)

**Demo backbone order** (live presentation, 3 beats only):
1. Perišić
2. VAR Nested (continues directly from Perišić — same incident, second layer)
3. Suárez

De Jong and Mbappé are fully built, fully navigable product content. They are not absent from the project — they are absent from the scripted live walkthrough specifically. Any document or script referring to "the demo" should use the 3-beat order; any document referring to "the product" or "the taxonomy" should use the 5-incident order. Conflating the two is what caused the original drift.
