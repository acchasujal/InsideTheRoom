# Judge Defense Playbook

This playbook outlines exactly how to respond to typical judge questions and critiques during the Q&A segment of the innovation challenge. Stick to the locked thesis: **Inside the Room is a Discretion Disclosure Instrument for governing text.**

## Critique 1: "Couldn't this just be a static website or database lookup?"
**The Vulnerability:** The core walkthrough runs on cached perspectives for safety, which risks looking like a simple database lookup.
**The Defense:** "For the 3-minute demo, we use a validated reference benchmark to ensure reliability, as any production system would. However, to prove Granite is structurally necessary and reasoning live, we built the Framing Sensitivity Test."
**Action:** *Immediately pivot to the Framing Sensitivity Test.* "Let's submit the exact same incident under two different phrasings. A static site can't do this. We show live, on-screen, that the model's output shifts with the phrasing. That is the discretion risk we govern, and it requires a live inference engine."

## Critique 2: "Why doesn't the AI just tell us the correct call?"
**The Vulnerability:** Judges often expect AI to be an oracle that solves human error.
**The Defense:** "Because the 'correct call' doesn't exist objectively. Our core thesis is that governing texts deliberately contain undefined words—like 'reckless' or 'deliberate'. The AI cannot solve an undefined term because the law refuses to define it. We are not using AI to replace the referee or the compliance officer; we use it to map the exact boundaries where the rules run out and judgment begins."

## Critique 3: "Are you predicting match outcomes or replacing refs?"
**The Vulnerability:** These are explicitly disqualified or low-scoring patterns in the challenge.
**The Defense:** "Absolutely not. This is a Discretion Disclosure Instrument. We are using football as a universally understood domain to demonstrate an enterprise compliance tool. The goal is to document interpretive variance and surface discretion risk, creating a compliance-grade artifact for regulators and stakeholders."

## Critique 4: "Why use IBM Granite for this? What's the specific value?"
**The Vulnerability:** Failure to demonstrate why *watsonx.ai* specifically is required.
**The Defense:** "Inside the Room maps directly onto watsonx.governance's core use case: making AI decision discretion visible, auditable, and reportable. Granite isn't just chatting; we instruct it to adopt 4 distinct interpretive schools (Purposive, Textualist, Procedural, Strict Constructionist) and argue against itself. It proves Granite's capability to safely navigate ambiguity and generate a transparent, compliance-ready Discretion Disclosure Report."

## Critique 5: "Where are the data visualizations or 3D reconstructions?"
**The Vulnerability:** Competitors will have flashy 3D pitch models.
**The Defense:** "Our competitors believe the problem is missing data—that if we just had better camera angles, we'd know the truth. We proved today that data isn't the problem. The words themselves are the problem. 3D models of a tackle don't help you define 'reckless.' We optimized for intellectual honesty and enterprise governance over decorative graphics."
