# Incident Content Registry

This registry provides the structured content required for the five canonical incidents.

## 1. Perišić Handball (Intent / Threshold Blend)
- **Incident Summary:** Ivan Perišić's arm makes contact with the ball in the penalty area during the 2018 World Cup Final.
- **FIFA Law Involved:** Law 12 (Fouls and Misconduct - Handling the ball).
- **Disputed Term:** "Deliberately" / "Unnaturally bigger".
- **Evidence Shown:** Video/images of the ball striking the arm; player's running posture.
- **Fan Perspective:** It's a clear penalty. His arm blocked the cross and gave an unfair advantage.
- **Referee Perspective:** The arm was in a natural position for a player jumping. There was no intent to handle the ball.
- **VAR Perspective:** The arm position was unnaturally bigger, creating a barrier. This is a clear and obvious error by the referee.
- **Rulebook Perspective:** It is an offense if a player deliberately touches the ball or makes their body unnaturally bigger.
- **Reveal:** The word "deliberately" appears four times in Law 12 and has no definition. "Unnaturally bigger" has no definition.
- **Decision 2 Prompt:** Now that you know the law doesn't define these terms, is it still a clear penalty?
- **Reflection:** The certainty of the first decision was built on undefined terms. Mental state is unobservable.
- **Emotional Objective:** Shock and realization that the rulebook is hollow at its core.

## 2. VAR Nested Layer (Judgment)
- **Incident Summary:** The VAR intervention mechanism regarding the Perišić handball.
- **FIFA Law Involved:** VAR Protocol.
- **Disputed Term:** "Clear and obvious error".
- **Evidence Shown:** The referee's initial "no penalty" call vs. the VAR recommendation to review and overturn.
- **Fan Perspective:** The initial call was completely wrong; VAR saved the game.
- **Referee Perspective:** It was a subjective, 50/50 call. It wasn't obviously wrong enough to warrant an overturn.
- **VAR Perspective:** The missed handball was a clear error that directly impacted a goal-scoring opportunity.
- **Rulebook Perspective:** VAR may only intervene in the event of a "clear and obvious error" or "serious missed incident."
- **Reveal:** "Clear and obvious" has no definition. The correction mechanism itself relies on an interpretive judgment.
- **Decision 2 Prompt:** Does a review mechanism based on an undefined term solve the ambiguity, or just move it?
- **Reflection:** We replaced an undefined foul with an undefined standard for correction.
- **Emotional Objective:** Epiphany about systemic ambiguity in officiating technology.

## 3. De Jong Chest Kick (Threshold)
- **Incident Summary:** Nigel de Jong kicks Xabi Alonso in the chest during the 2010 World Cup Final.
- **FIFA Law Involved:** Law 12 (Fouls and Misconduct).
- **Disputed Term:** "Serious foul play" (excessive force) vs. "reckless" (yellow card).
- **Evidence Shown:** The high boot to the chest. Referee Howard Webb issuing a yellow card, and his later quote stating it should have been red.
- **Fan Perspective:** It's assault. A clear red card in any league in the world.
- **Referee Perspective:** In the context of a World Cup Final, it looked reckless but lacked the malicious intent required to ruin the game with a red card.
- **VAR Perspective:** The player used excessive force and endangered the safety of an opponent. It is serious foul play.
- **Rulebook Perspective:** Reckless challenges warrant a yellow card. Using excessive force constitutes serious foul play (red card).
- **Reveal:** Howard Webb gave a yellow, but years later said it should have been red. Same referee, same footage, two answers. The boundary between "reckless" and "excessive force" is undefined.
- **Decision 2 Prompt:** If the same referee gives two different answers to the exact same footage, what is the "correct" call?
- **Reflection:** The boundary between a caution and a sending-off is fundamentally undefined.
- **Emotional Objective:** Resignation to the impossibility of objective consistency.

## 4. Mbappé "Deliberate Play" Offside (Scope)
- **Incident Summary:** Kylian Mbappé receives the ball in an offside position, but a defender's touch resets the play, allowing him to score in the 2021 Nations League Final.
- **FIFA Law Involved:** Law 11 (Offside).
- **Disputed Term:** "Deliberately played".
- **Evidence Shown:** Mbappé's position, the pass, the defender's attempt to intercept, and the resulting goal.
- **Fan Perspective:** He was miles offside when the ball was kicked! The goal shouldn't stand.
- **Referee Perspective:** The defender intentionally moved to intercept the ball. This constitutes a deliberate play, resetting the offside phase.
- **VAR Perspective:** The defender's action meets the criteria for a deliberate play. The offside offense is nullified.
- **Rulebook Perspective:** A player in an offside position receiving the ball from an opponent who deliberately played the ball is not considered to have gained an advantage.
- **Reveal:** "Deliberately played" has no definition. Its guidance terms (e.g., "in control") are also undefined. It is undefined all the way down.
- **Decision 2 Prompt:** Can you enforce an offside rule if the condition that resets it is undefined?
- **Reflection:** The law defines its very area of application using undefined terms.
- **Emotional Objective:** Frustration with recursive bureaucratic ambiguity.

## 5. Suárez Goal-Line Handball (Context)
- **Incident Summary:** Luis Suárez intentionally handles the ball on the goal line to prevent a certain goal for Ghana in the 2010 World Cup Quarter-Final.
- **FIFA Law Involved:** Law 12 (Fouls and Misconduct).
- **Disputed Term:** Adequacy of punishment / Procedural vs. Substantive Justice.
- **Evidence Shown:** The deliberate handball, the red card, the missed penalty, and Uruguay advancing.
- **Fan Perspective:** He cheated, broke the rules intentionally, and his team was rewarded with a trip to the semi-finals.
- **Referee Perspective:** I applied the law perfectly. I sent the player off and awarded a penalty kick. That is the maximum punishment allowed.
- **VAR Perspective:** The referee correctly identified the denial of an obvious goal-scoring opportunity by handball. Red card and penalty are the correct sanctions.
- **Rulebook Perspective:** Denying the opposing team a goal or an obvious goal-scoring opportunity by a handball offense results in a sending-off and a penalty kick.
- **Reveal:** The law doesn't define 'adequate remedy.' It doesn't define 'justice.' It only defines procedure. Every rule was followed perfectly. Ghana still went home.
- **Decision 2 Prompt:** Was justice served?
- **Reflection:** The law is completely silent on whether its remedy fits the context of the situation.
- **Emotional Objective:** A deep sense of unfairness and an understanding of the fundamental limits of the law.
